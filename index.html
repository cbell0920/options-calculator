📌 Underlying Price (S)

The current stock price. Find it on any quote page — Yahoo Finance, Thinkorswim, Robinhood. Use the last price or mid of bid/ask.

AAPL is trading at $211.50 → enter 211.50

📌 Strike Price (K)

The price you have the right to buy (call) or sell (put) the stock at. Choose this from the options chain in your broker. ATM = closest to current price.

Buying the $215 call → enter 215

📌 Days to Expiry (DTE)

Calendar days until the option expires. Count from today to the expiration date. Most retail traders use 21–45 DTE for buying options.

Expiry is 3 weeks away → enter 21
Tip: shorter DTE = faster decay (Theta). Under 7 DTE is high risk for buyers.

📌 IV % — Implied Volatility

This is shown on your broker's options chain, usually labeled IV or Impl. Vol. It's already annualized — enter the number as-is. High IV = expensive premium.

Options chain shows IV = 32.4% → enter 32.4
Where to find it: Thinkorswim options chain → IV column. Yahoo Finance → Options tab → "Implied Volatility" per strike. Robinhood shows it on the option detail screen.

📌 Risk-Free Rate %

The current US Treasury yield — used as the "guaranteed" return rate in the Black-Scholes formula. Pre-filled at 5.25%. You rarely need to change this unless rates have moved significantly.

Fed funds rate is ~5.25% → leave as-is
Current rate: check Google for "10-year treasury yield" or use the Fed Funds rate. Small changes here have minimal impact on the calculated price.

📌 Dividend Yield %

The stock's annual dividend as a percentage of its price. Dividends reduce call value and increase put value slightly. Leave at 0 for non-dividend stocks.

WMT pays ~1.3% annual dividend → enter 1.3
AAPL pays ~0.5% → enter 0.5
SPY ETF pays ~1.3% → enter 1.3
ONDS pays no dividend → leave 0
Find it on Yahoo Finance → Summary page → "Forward Dividend & Yield".

📌 Market Premium (Ask Price)

The actual price being charged for the option in the market — the ask price on the options chain. Enter this to get the fair / rich / cheap verdict comparing market price to Black-Scholes fair value.

The $215 call is asking $3.45 → enter 3.45
If the market price is more than ~10% above BS fair value, implied volatility is elevated — you're paying a premium for uncertainty.

📌 Reading the Results

Fair Value — what Black-Scholes says the option is worth.
Break-Even — stock must reach this price at expiry for you to profit.
Prob ITM — statistical chance of expiring in the money.
Delta — how much the option moves per $1 in the stock.
Theta — dollars you lose per day just from time passing.
Expected Move — the range the market prices in by expiry (1 standard deviation).
